📖 Documentation — FDA 483 Observation Prevention

How This Tool Prevents FDA 483 Observations

FDA 483 observations are issued when investigators identify conditions that may constitute violations of the Food Drug & Cosmetic Act and related statutes, including DSCSA §582. The SGS-Sentinel Technical Audit Sandbox catches non-interoperable data before it reaches a trading partner's EPCIS repository — the #1 root cause of failed T-STAMP queries and interoperability breakdowns that trigger FDA scrutiny.

Pre-Transmission Validation: The First Line of Defence

By validating EPCIS 2.0 JSON-LD payloads in the Audit Sandbox, organisations catch CBV 2.0 vocabulary errors, missing mandatory fields (eventTime, bizStep, epcList), and structural defects before data is published to the production EPCIS repository. This prevents corrupt events from propagating across the supply chain and causing cascading T-STAMP failures at trading partner nodes — a scenario that triggers §582(b)(4)(A) non-compliance.

Resolution Workflow: Correcting Supply Chain Deadlocks

When a CBV semantic error or missing disposition field creates an unresolvable exception in the trading partner network, the Resolution Workflow generates a standards-compliant CorrectionEvent payload. This enables supply chain actors to clear deadlocked transactions without manual re-keying — a process that introduces data integrity risks and often results in FDA 483 observations for inadequate record-keeping per §582(e)(4).

Digital Signature Integrity: DSCSA 2026 Data Provenance

DSCSA 2026 guidance anticipates data integrity requirements for EPCIS event chains. The SHA-256 digital signature simulation demonstrates cryptographic hashing of validated events with tamper-evident timestamps — a control that satisfies the FDA expectation for immutable, verifiable audit trails (per §582(e)(4) 6-year retention mandate). Organisations with demonstrable hash-chain integrity controls receive fewer 483 observations related to data provenance deficiencies.
